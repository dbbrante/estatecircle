Custom pools in Miami — what to expect and how to choose the right builder

Estate Circle Journal  ·  Miami, Florida

A custom pool is one of the most transformative upgrades you can make to a Miami estate. Done well, it becomes the centrepiece of your outdoor living space — a place for entertaining, unwinding, and making the most of South Florida's year-round climate. Done poorly, it becomes a source of stress, delays and unexpected costs.

This guide covers everything a luxury homeowner in Miami needs to know before starting a custom pool project — from realistic budgets and timelines to design options and how to find a builder you can trust.

What does a custom pool cost in Miami?

Budget is the first question most homeowners ask, and the honest answer is that it varies enormously. A well-designed custom pool at a luxury Miami estate typically starts at $100,000 and can easily reach $500,000 or more depending on size, materials, features and site conditions.

The key cost drivers are pool size and shape, the materials used for the shell and finish, additional features like integrated spas, water features, LED lighting and smart automation systems, the quality of surrounding decking and landscaping, and site complexity. A custom pool is also one of the estate improvements with the strongest return on value in South Florida — particularly on waterfront properties in Miami Beach or Star Island where soil conditions and permitting requirements add meaningful cost.

For a truly bespoke pool at a luxury estate in Miami Beach or Coral Gables, budget a minimum of $150,000 and expect the process to take six to twelve months from design to completion.

Design options for Miami estates

Miami's luxury pool market has evolved significantly. The most sought-after designs today reflect a blend of resort-style aesthetics and the specific demands of South Florida's climate and lifestyle.

Infinity edge pools remain the premier choice for waterfront properties in areas like Star Island, Miami Beach and the Venetian Islands, where the visual effect of water meeting the horizon is unmatched. For inland estates in Coral Gables or Coconut Grove, geometric pools with clean lines and large format limestone or travertine decking are consistently popular.

Integrated spa areas, shallow sun shelves, and automated water features controllable via smartphone are now standard expectations at the top end of the market. So is LED colour lighting, which transforms the pool at night — particularly effective in properties with significant landscape lighting. Many homeowners pair a new pool with an outdoor kitchen and entertaining area or a whole-home smart system to complete the outdoor living experience.

For the pool finish, pebble and quartz aggregate surfaces have largely replaced traditional plaster at the luxury level, offering better durability and a richer visual texture. Glass tile accents remain a hallmark of high-end work — they add significantly to the build cost but deliver a result that is visually exceptional.

Infinity edge pools are particularly suited to Miami's waterfront estates.

Timelines — what to expect

One of the most common sources of frustration for Miami homeowners is underestimating the timeline. A custom pool is not a quick project.

The permitting process in Miami-Dade and Broward counties alone can take two to four months. Design and engineering adds another four to eight weeks. Construction itself — once permits are in hand and the contractor is mobilised — takes three to five months for a typical luxury pool. Factor in weather delays, supply chain lead times or any change orders, and a six to twelve month timeline from first conversation to first swim is realistic.

The best builders will be transparent about this from the outset. Be cautious of any contractor who promises an unrealistically fast completion — it is almost always a sign that something will be cut.

How to choose the right pool builder in Miami

The quality of pool builders in Miami varies widely. The luxury segment has a small number of firms that consistently deliver exceptional work, and a much larger number of contractors who market themselves as luxury builders but fall short in execution.

When evaluating builders, look closely at their portfolio — specifically projects in Miami at a comparable budget to yours. Ask for references from past clients and actually call them. Verify that they hold a valid Florida Certified Pool and Spa Contractor licence. Look for membership in the Association of Pool and Spa Professionals (APSP) as a signal of professional standards.

Be cautious of the lowest bid. Custom pool projects at the luxury level are complex, and a builder who dramatically undercuts the competition is typically cutting corners somewhere — in materials, in supervision, or in the quality of sub-contractors they use. The cost of remedying poor workmanship almost always exceeds the original savings.

Frequently Asked Questions

How much does a custom pool cost in Miami?

A custom pool at a luxury Miami estate typically starts at $100,000 and can reach $500,000 or more depending on size, materials and features. For a bespoke pool with spa, water features and premium finishes in areas like Miami Beach or Coral Gables, budgeting $150,000–$300,000 is realistic.

How long does it take to build a custom pool in Miami?

From first conversation to completion, expect six to twelve months. Permitting in Miami-Dade typically takes two to four months. Design and engineering adds four to eight weeks. Construction takes three to five months once permits are approved.

Do I need a permit to build a pool in Miami?

Yes. All pool construction in Miami-Dade County requires building permits. Your contractor should handle the permitting process but factor two to four months into your overall project timeline.

What is the best pool type for a Miami waterfront estate?

Infinity edge pools are the premier choice for waterfront properties, creating a visual effect of water meeting the horizon. For inland estates, geometric pools with large format stone decking are most popular. Integrated spas, sun shelves and smart automation are standard at the luxury level.
