How to build an infinity pool at a luxury estate

Estate Circle Journal

An infinity pool is among the most striking features a luxury estate can have. The vanishing edge — water appearing to merge with the horizon, the ocean or the surrounding landscape — is an engineering achievement as much as a design one. Getting it right requires the right team, not just the right vision.

This guide covers who builds infinity pools for luxury estates, what the project actually involves, what it costs, and why so many homeowners underestimate the complexity until they are already committed.

Why infinity pools require specialist builders

An infinity pool — also known as a vanishing edge or negative edge pool — is significantly more complex to build than a standard pool. Water flows continuously over one or more edges into a hidden catch basin, where it is filtered and recirculated back into the pool. The engineering required to make this work correctly involves precise hydraulic calculations, structural design of the edge wall and catch basin, and careful attention to water chemistry and flow rates.

Many pool companies build standard pools. Far fewer have the specific experience to engineer a vanishing edge correctly. A pool built by a contractor without genuine infinity pool experience will almost always develop problems — uneven flow over the edge, catch basin overflow in wind conditions, or structural cracking from inadequate support. The difference between a specialist and a generalist is visible every time you look at the water.

For a luxury estate, you want a high-end pool contractor who specifically has infinity pool experience. Many pool companies build pools — very few engineer vanishing edges.

The team a serious infinity pool project requires

A well-executed infinity pool at the luxury level involves more than a pool builder. Understanding the full team required — and why each role matters — is essential before you begin.

The custom pool builder is the lead contractor, responsible for the structural shell, the vanishing edge system, plumbing, filtration, hydraulics and all finishing work including tiling and coping. They should have a specific portfolio of completed vanishing edge projects, not just standard pools. Ask to see ten or more examples of completed infinity pools before committing.

A structural engineer is required for any infinity pool on a slope, elevated position or near a property boundary. The catch basin wall that receives the overflow water must be engineered to handle the continuous hydraulic load — this is not a calculation a pool builder makes alone. Skipping the structural engineer is one of the most common and most expensive mistakes in this category.

For luxury estates where the pool is a centrepiece of the grounds, a landscape architect or landscape designer integrates the pool with the surrounding terracing, planting, outdoor kitchen and living areas. An infinity pool that sits well in the landscape — aligned with the horizon, framed by the right planting, connected to a coherent outdoor living space — is a completely different experience from one that simply functions correctly.

Site and orientation — the decisions that cannot be undone

The position and orientation of an infinity pool are the most consequential decisions in the project, and the ones that are hardest to reverse once construction has begun. The vanishing edge should be aligned with the most compelling view available from the pool — whether that is ocean, bay, cityscape or open landscape. The edge that disappears should face that view.

Slope is an advantage for an infinity pool, not an obstacle. A site that drops away from the house creates a natural opportunity for the catch basin to sit below the pool level, making the hydraulic engineering cleaner and the visual effect more dramatic. A flat site requires more engineering to achieve the same result.

Sun orientation matters both for the swimming experience and for the visual effect of the edge. An infinity pool that catches the afternoon sun on its vanishing edge — the light breaking across the water as it flows over — is a different proposition from one that faces north. A good landscape architect and pool designer will consider this from the outset.

What does an infinity pool cost?

A well-specified vanishing edge pool at a luxury estate typically costs $60,000–$150,000 for the pool itself. This range assumes a custom concrete and gunite shell, quality tiling, a properly engineered catch basin, premium filtration and automation, and standard site conditions.

At the higher end of the market — with large format stone coping, glass tile, an integrated spa or hot tub, water features such as jets or deck-level overflows, automated LED lighting, and comprehensive landscaping integration — costs typically reach $200,000–$500,000 or more for the complete outdoor installation.

As with all estate projects, plan for a 20–30% contingency above initial estimates. Site conditions — particularly on elevated or sloped properties — can introduce structural requirements that affect cost significantly. A specialist pool contractor will conduct a thorough site assessment before providing a final number.

Smart home and automation integration

A luxury infinity pool at an estate level should integrate with the property's wider smart home automation system. Pool temperature, filtration schedules, lighting scenes and water feature controls should all be accessible from the same interface used to control the rest of the estate — whether that is Control4, Crestron or a dedicated pool automation system such as Pentair or Jandy.

Specifying pool automation at the design stage, rather than retrofitting it after completion, produces a cleaner installation and avoids the additional cost of running conduit through finished hardscape. This is a conversation to have with both the pool builder and the AV integrator at the outset of the project.

Frequently Asked Questions

How much does an infinity pool cost at a luxury estate?

A well-specified vanishing edge pool typically costs $60,000–$150,000. With integrated spa, stone coping, water features, lighting and full landscaping, costs can reach $200,000–$500,000 or more for the complete outdoor installation.

Who builds infinity pools for luxury homes?

Infinity pools require a specialised custom pool builder with specific vanishing edge experience, a structural engineer for the catch basin design, and typically a landscape architect to integrate the pool with the surrounding grounds.

What is the difference between an infinity pool and a regular pool?

An infinity pool has one or more edges where water flows over into a hidden catch basin and is recirculated, creating the illusion that the pool merges with the horizon. The engineering is significantly more complex than a standard pool, requiring specialist hydraulic design and structural calculations.

How long does it take to build an infinity pool?

A custom infinity pool typically takes four to nine months from design sign-off to completion, depending on site conditions, structural complexity and the scope of surrounding landscaping. Permitting should be factored in from the outset.

What should I look for in an infinity pool builder?

Look for a builder with a specific portfolio of completed vanishing edge pools, not just standard pools. Confirm they work with a structural engineer on the catch basin design and ask specifically about their hydraulic engineering capability.
